BiciLab

About the project

BiciLab is a design laboratory that aims to conduct visualization experiments to create meaningful interactions between urban cyclists and their environment. The lab was created to bridge the gap between data, technology and the lived experience of cycling in a city.

Through a series of design lab sessions, we developed a visualization module that collects sensor data from cyclists and transforms it into real-time visual feedback — helping riders understand their environment in new ways.

BiciLab's purpose is to use design as a tool for urban research: making the invisible visible, and empowering cyclists with information about their own trajectories.

The lab

We faced real technological challenges in the field: sensors that had to withstand vibration, heat and rain while still transmitting clean data. The lab sessions were iterative — each prototype informed the next.

The technology module/probe

The technology probe is a small Arduino-based device mounted on the bicycle. It collects GPS coordinates, speed, ambient sound levels and air quality data in real time, transmitting everything to a visualization dashboard.
